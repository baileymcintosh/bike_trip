# Charity: Sports & Community Centers for Underserved Neighborhoods

This section covers the charitable giving side of the bike trip across America. The goal is to raise money for an evidence-backed cause while riding — and to be thoughtful about where that money goes so it has the greatest possible impact.

---

## The Cause

The chosen cause is **sports and community center access in low-income neighborhoods**.

The intuition is simple: these spaces bring communities together in an active, structured, positive way. They give kids somewhere to be after school, mentors to look up to, and an alternative to the street. The research backs this up — access to quality community programming is associated with reduced crime, lower obesity rates, better educational outcomes, and improved long-term earnings.

The case for this cause from an effective altruism standpoint:
- The intervention is **proven**, not speculative — structured youth programs with mentorship have measurable positive effects in peer-reviewed literature.
- The **counterfactual matters** — in many low-income neighborhoods, there is simply nothing else. A donation that funds a Boys & Girls Club in a high-poverty area may be the only structured after-school option for hundreds of kids.
- The **cost per life improved** compares favorably to other domestic causes, especially when you consider the downstream effects (crime avoidance, better health, higher earnings) that compound over a lifetime.

## Donation Strategy

The cleanest narrative for the fundraiser: **Philadelphia to Los Angeles — every dollar goes to communities connected to this ride, from start to finish.**

Suggested split:

| Org | Role | % |
|---|---|---|
| World Bicycle Relief | Global cycling anchor — $165/bike, RCT evidence | 25% |
| Up2Us Sports | National — scale and credibility | 25% |
| Philadelphia Youth Sports Collaborative | Local — where the ride begins | 25% |
| Heart of Los Angeles | Local — where the ride ends | 25% |

Other approaches worth considering:

1. **Milestone giving**: Pledge per mile, or unlock donations at checkpoints (day 1, the Mississippi, the Rockies, the Pacific). Gives followers a reason to stay engaged throughout.
2. **Match challenge**: Ask an employer or contact to match donations up to a cap. Matching doubles impact and dramatically increases donor motivation.
3. **Penn network**: As a UPenn student, PYSC is a natural ask for Penn alumni and faculty donors who care about Philadelphia — leverage that connection.

## Why This, Not Something Else?

From an effective altruism perspective, the most impactful global health interventions (malaria nets, deworming) often rank higher on pure cost-per-life-saved metrics. This cause is chosen for different but legitimate reasons:

- **Direct connection to the ride**: Crossing America, passing through cities where this problem is visible, creates a natural narrative.
- **Domestic focus**: Some donors prefer to give domestically, and this is one of the most evidence-backed domestic causes.
- **Systemic leverage**: Community centers don't just help one kid — they change the social fabric of a neighborhood, which generates compounding effects across generations.

If you want to go purely global-health EA, check [GiveWell's top charities](https://www.givewell.org/charities/top-charities). But if you want a domestic cause with strong evidence, community sports programming is one of the best options available.
