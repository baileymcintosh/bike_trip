# Charity Recommendations: Sports & Community Centers for Underserved Communities

This document evaluates charities to support as part of this bike trip fundraiser. The goal is to fund organizations that bring sports and community center access to low-income neighborhoods — an intervention with strong evidence for reducing crime, improving health, and expanding opportunity.

The portfolio is built around a **global cycling anchor + national org + local bookends** model: one global charity with direct thematic connection to the ride, one national US org for scale, one Philadelphia org (start), and one LA org (finish).

---

## Evaluation Framework

When choosing where to donate, three things matter most for effective giving:

1. **Evidence of impact** — does the organization have credible outcome data?
2. **Transparency and accountability** — top ratings from Charity Navigator, GiveWell, or CharityWatch?
3. **Counterfactual impact** — does the organization reach kids who truly lack access, not just those who would find another program?

## Recommended Portfolio

### Global Cycling Anchor — World Bicycle Relief
**Website:** [worldbicyclerelief.org](https://worldbicyclerelief.org)  
**Charity Navigator:** 4 stars (94% score)  
**Focus:** Providing purpose-built bicycles to students, healthcare workers, and farmers in rural sub-Saharan Africa and other developing regions

The thematic fit with a cross-country bike trip is obvious, but the impact case is what makes this compelling. A single bike costs **$165** to deliver and transforms a child's daily life: rural students in Zambia often walk 3+ hours each way to school; a bike cuts that to under an hour. The result is better attendance, fewer dropouts, and improved grades.

**The evidence is exceptional for a charity of this type.** WBR commissioned a rigorous **randomized controlled trial (RCT)** — the gold standard in social science — through Innovations for Poverty Action (IPA) and IDinsight. Results for girls who received bicycles in Zambia:
- **19% less likely to drop out of school**
- **28% fewer absences**
- **66% more likely to arrive on time**
- Higher scores on math assessments
- Greater personal aspirations, higher self-image, and a greater desire to delay marriage

88% of students in BEEP (Bicycles for Educational Empowerment) program schools complete their schooling, versus a 60% national average. Income gains for households with access to a bike run up to 35%.

**Scale:** 850,000+ bikes distributed to date; 4 million+ people impacted.  
**Why it stands out:** The RCT evidence is rare in this space and puts WBR in a different category of credibility. The $165/bike cost means fundraising milestones are highly concrete — every $165 raised is one bike, one child's commute transformed. That's a powerful message for donors following the ride.

**The pitch:** *"I'm crossing America by bike to fund bikes for kids in rural Africa who need them to get to school."* The symmetry between your activity and the cause is unique and memorable.

### National Anchor — Up2Us Sports
**Website:** [up2us.org](https://www.up2us.org)  
**Charity Navigator:** 4 stars  
**Focus:** Training AmeriCorps coaches to work in underserved urban communities

Up2Us Sports is arguably the most evidence-aligned organization in this space. It recruits young adults from targeted communities, trains them in sports-based youth development and trauma-sensitive coaching, and places them in low-income neighborhoods. External evaluation found statistically significant improvements in youth physical fitness and psychosocial outcomes including discipline, goal-setting, and social connections.

**Scale:** Since 2009, more than 3,900 coaches trained; nearly 450,000 youth reached.  
**Why it stands out:** The AmeriCorps model means federal co-funding leverages private donations — your dollar stretches further. The coaching-first approach is scalable and reaches kids regardless of whether a physical facility exists. Up2Us Sports also has an active partnership with Philadelphia Youth Sports Collaborative, so there's a direct connection to the Philly scene.

### Philadelphia — Philadelphia Youth Sports Collaborative (PYSC)
**Website:** [pysc.org](https://pysc.org)  
**Charity Navigator:** 4 stars (90% score)  
**Focus:** Equitable access to youth sports across Philadelphia; system-building and direct programs

PYSC is the hub of Philadelphia's youth sports ecosystem. It doesn't just run programs — it coordinates across government, hospitals, schools, professional sports teams, and community orgs to build a city-wide infrastructure for youth sports access. Their Youth Engagement in Sports (YES) program has run in over 200 low-income school communities with outcomes tracked via a formal research study. They also measure Social and Emotional Learning (SEL) outcomes across all member organizations.

**Recent milestone:** Their five-year citywide plan, "Game On Philly!", is backed by a coalition spanning government, higher education, health, and philanthropy.  
**Local impact:** Only 20% of Philadelphia's kids meet the CDC's recommended 60 minutes/day of activity — PYSC is working to close that gap at scale.  
**UPenn connection:** PYSC works closely with Philadelphia-based universities. As a Penn student, you may be able to engage beyond just donating — volunteering, fundraising through Penn networks, or even connecting PYSC with Penn's social impact programs.

**Alternatives in Philly worth knowing:**
- [Team Up Philly](https://www.teamupphilly.org) — free sports and enrichment specifically for girls in underserved Philadelphia communities
- [Starfinder Foundation](https://starfinderfoundation.org) — serves youth in underrepresented communities in Manayunk/Northwest Philly
- [Leveling the Playing Field](https://www.levelingtheplayingfield.org/locations/philadelphia-pa/) — redistributes free sports equipment to schools and orgs that can't afford it

### Los Angeles — Heart of Los Angeles (HOLA)
**Website:** [heartofla.org](https://heartofla.org)  
**Charity Navigator:** 4 stars, **8 consecutive years**  
**Focus:** After-school academic, arts, athletics, and wellness programs in LA's highest-need neighborhoods

HOLA operates in MacArthur Park/Westlake, South Central, and Watts — some of the most underserved communities in the country. Over 35 years it has served 50,000+ kids, with 3,100+ enrolled annually across 330+ weekly classes. The outcomes are strong: 90% of HOLA's elementary students perform at or above grade level, and 97%+ of high school students go on to succeed post-graduation.

**Why it stands out:** Eight consecutive 4-star Charity Navigator ratings signals genuine, sustained accountability. HOLA is a full wraparound program — not just sports, but academics, arts, and health — which reflects the research finding that multi-component programs have the strongest outcomes. Finishing the ride in LA and having HOLA as the local beneficiary gives the fundraiser a compelling narrative arc.

**Alternatives in LA worth knowing:**
- [LA84 Foundation](https://la84.org) — grants engine for 3,300+ Southern California youth sports orgs; strong if you want to support the broader ecosystem
- [Kids In Sports](https://www.idealist.org/en/nonprofit/9c49dd94655e4e0aa07ae0d6440fa9b6-kids-in-sports-los-angeles) — free/affordable sports clubs for 8,500+ kids in LA County

## Other Strong National Options

If you want to vary the national anchor or split further across orgs:

### Boys & Girls Clubs of America (BGCA)
**Website:** [bgca.org](https://www.bgca.org)  
**Charity Navigator:** 4 stars (Platinum Seal of Transparency)  
**CharityWatch:** Top-rated | **Forbes:** #1 youth charity (2025)

The household name in this space — 5,500+ clubs, 4 million+ children served annually. Both Philadelphia and LA have local BGCA chapters. Strong governance, decades of outcome data, rated by every major watchdog. If donors want to give to a name they recognize, BGCA is the answer. Donating to a local club chapter rather than the national office maximizes direct impact.

### YMCA (Local Chapters)
**Website:** [ymca.org](https://www.ymca.org)  
**Charity Navigator:** Multiple local branches at 4 stars

The largest community center network in the US. Sliding-scale fees mean no child is turned away for inability to pay. Both Philadelphia and LA have Y branches serving high-need neighborhoods.

### Every Kid Sports
**Website:** [everykidsports.org](https://everykidsports.org)

Pays registration fees directly so low-income kids can join leagues they otherwise can't afford. Simple model, easy to explain to donors. 20,000+ kids served in 2022.
